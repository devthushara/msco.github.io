The Legacy

From the Bank Vault to the Cinnamon Garden

The story of Matara Spice Co. begins with R.A. Lionel Ranawaka. After decades as a Deputy Chief Financial Officer at a major Sri Lankan state bank, Lionel returned to his roots in Malimbada, Matara—the ancestral home of true cinnamon.

What started as a retirement passion on a 4-acre estate soon revealed exceptional quality. Lionel applied the same meticulous attention to detail required in high-level finance to the art of cultivation. His sons, Thushara (Asia) and Ishara (Australia), joined forces to turn this passion into a global venture.

The 50% Profit Pledge

We believe in ethical partnership, not just employment. We proudly share 50% of peeling profits directly with the skilled local artisans who craft our quills. This ensures sustainability for the community in Malimbada.

Our Future Vision: We are expanding our cultivation to include a boutique cinnamon experience villa, where visitors can eventually join us to harvest, peel, and process oil firsthand.

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about our spice origin, shipping, and usage.

What is the difference between your Ceylon Cinnamon and regular Cassia?

The difference is critical for health. Cassia (common supermarket cinnamon) contains high levels of Coumarin (~1%), a compound linked to liver damage if consumed daily.

Our Ceylon Cinnamon (Cinnamomum verum) contains ultra-low Coumarin traces (~0.004%), making it safe for daily wellness routines. It also has a sweeter, more delicate flavor profile compared to the harsh heat of Cassia.

Do you ship internationally? How long does it take?

Yes, we ship worldwide from Colombo, Sri Lanka.

  • Express (DHL/FedEx): 3-7 business days. Ideal for samples and retail orders.
  • Air Freight: 7-10 days. Best for medium wholesale orders (50kg+).
  • Sea Freight: 25-40 days depending on the destination port. Most cost-effective for bulk pallet orders.
We handle all export documentation, including Phytosanitary Certificates and Certificates of Origin.

Is your cinnamon Organic certified?

Yes. We use strictly traditional farming methods on our Malimbada estate. We do not use synthetic pesticides or chemical fertilizers. For bulk B2B orders, we can provide third-party lab analysis reports confirming the purity and organic status of the batch.

What is the shelf life of your products?

Because we mill and pack fresh at the source, our shelf life is superior to retail brands that sit in warehouses for months.

  • Whole Quills: 3-4 years if stored in a cool, dry place.
  • Powder: 1-2 years. We recommend buying smaller batches of powder or grinding quills yourself for maximum potency.

How should I store cinnamon to keep it fresh?

Moisture and light are the enemies of spice. Store your cinnamon in an airtight glass or tin container in a cool, dark cupboard. Do not store it directly over the stove, as heat and humidity will degrade the volatile oils that give cinnamon its flavor.

What's the difference between fresh and dried turmeric/ginger?

Fresh (Rhizome): Ideal for juices, smoothies, and fresh applications. Higher moisture content, vibrant flavor, shorter shelf life. Must be refrigerated or frozen. Perfect for wellness shots and fresh cooking.

Dried (Powder): Long shelf life, concentrated flavor, easier to store and ship. Perfect for curries, golden milk, and baking. Our dried powders are made from premium rhizomes, sun-dried and finely ground.

We can supply both forms based on your needs and shipping requirements.
